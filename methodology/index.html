🧠 Trading Scanner Methodology

The scanners are designed to identify short-term swing-trading candidates using a structured scoring model. The objective is not to predict markets perfectly, but to filter for setups with better momentum, trend strength, liquidity, and risk-reward structure.

Core Objective

The system looks for trade candidates that may perform over a 1-to-10 trading day horizon. It is designed for swing-trade analysis, not long-term investing, intraday scalping, or guaranteed signal generation.

Primary Scoring Factors

Trend Strength

Preference is given to stocks showing constructive price structure and positive short-term trend behavior.

Momentum

The scanner looks for securities showing recent upward price acceleration or improving relative behavior.

Volume Confirmation

Higher-than-normal participation helps confirm whether a move is supported by meaningful market interest.

Risk Structure

Setups are evaluated for downside risk, stop-loss distance, and whether the expected upside justifies the trade.

Priority Grading

Scanner results are ranked using a total score. Higher-scoring names should generally represent cleaner technical setups, stronger momentum, better volume behavior, and more attractive risk-reward balance.

  • Highest Priority: Strongest candidates based on the scanner score.
  • Medium Priority: Good candidates, but with some weaker confirmation factors.
  • Low Priority: Watchlist candidates that may require additional confirmation.
  • Avoid / No Trade: Weak score, poor setup quality, or unattractive risk-reward.

Risk Management Logic

The scanner is intended to highlight possible opportunities, but the user must still control position size, stop-loss discipline, market exposure, and portfolio concentration.

  • Every trade idea should have a defined stop-loss before entry.
  • Risk per trade should remain limited and pre-decided.
  • Trade candidates should not be entered blindly only because they rank highly.
  • Market-wide weakness can override individual stock strength.

Performance Validation

The performance dashboard is designed to test whether the scanners are useful over time. A 10-trading-day window helps evaluate whether top-ranked candidates actually move in the expected direction.

  • Track top picks from each scan.
  • Measure return after the tracking period.
  • Record whether the target or stop-loss was hit.
  • Compare high-priority picks against lower-priority picks.
  • Use rolling results to improve the scanner rules.

Important Limitations

No scanner can remove market risk. Technical setups can fail because of broader market weakness, earnings news, macro events, liquidity shifts, or sudden sentiment changes.

  • The scanner should be treated as a filtering system, not a prediction engine.
  • Results may change significantly from one market session to another.
  • Backtesting and live forward-testing are both required before relying on any model.
  • Human judgment remains necessary for final trade selection.
