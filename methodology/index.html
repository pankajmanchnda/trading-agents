🧠 Trading Scanner Methodology

This methodology explains how the Indian and USA scanners identify, score, rank, and track short-term trading ideas. The scanners are designed as research and filtering tools. They do not predict certainty, and they do not remove market risk.

1. Core Objective

The system looks for short-term trade candidates using two separate views: Swing and Intraday. Swing mode uses daily candles and is designed for multi-day trade monitoring. Intraday mode uses 15-minute candles and is designed for faster active-trade monitoring.

The objective is not to produce guaranteed recommendations. The objective is to filter a broad stock universe into a smaller, risk-aware list of candidates with trend alignment, relative strength or weakness, volume confirmation, controlled volatility, and a defined entry, stop, and target.

2. Scanner Modes

Swing Mode

Uses daily candles. Best suited for 1-to-10 trading-day setups where the trigger, stop-loss, and target are monitored after entry.

Intraday Mode

Uses 15-minute candles. Best suited for faster monitoring where the setup must be closer to the trigger and supported by stronger near-term volume.

Indian Scanner

Uses NIFTY as the primary benchmark. If index data is unavailable, the scanner may use a NIFTY proxy such as NIFTYBEES.

USA Scanner

Uses SPY as the broad-market benchmark. Stocks with earnings expected within the blackout window are excluded from fresh picks.

3. Market Bias Filter

The scanner first checks the broad-market benchmark. A supportive benchmark allows more directional setups. A weak benchmark filters against bullish setups. A mixed benchmark requires stronger trade quality.

  • Bullish market bias: Scanner prefers BUY setups only.
  • Bearish market bias: Scanner prefers SELL setups only.
  • Mixed market bias: Scanner only allows stronger qualified setups with stricter confirmation.

4. Setup Scoring Factors

Trend Alignment

The model checks whether price is aligned with short-term and medium-term moving averages and whether the trend slope supports the trade direction.

Relative Strength

BUY setups should outperform the benchmark. SELL setups should underperform the benchmark. This helps avoid weak ideas in strong markets and strong names in weak markets.

Volume Quality

Setups receive higher quality treatment only when participation is above normal. Low-volume moves are less reliable and are filtered more aggressively.

RSI Zone

The scanner avoids extreme readings. It prefers healthier momentum zones rather than chasing severely overbought or oversold moves.

Volatility Control

ATR is used to check whether the stock has enough movement potential without being too erratic for controlled position sizing.

Trigger Proximity

The scanner favors candidates close to their entry trigger. Ideas too far from entry are less actionable and may be filtered out.

5. Conviction Score: What It Means

The conviction score is a setup-quality ranking, not a win probability. A score of 94 does not mean a 94% chance of winning. It means that, compared with the scanned universe, the setup has strong technical quality under the model’s rules.

No live trade is allowed to display a 100 conviction score. The scanners intentionally avoid 100 scores because no trade is loss-proof.
Score Range Priority Label Interpretation
90–94 Highest Priority Strongest technical setups of the scan, still requiring trigger confirmation.
84–89 Medium Priority Good setups, but with one or more weaker confirmation factors.
78–83 Low Priority Watchlist candidates that require stricter confirmation before consideration.
Below 78 Rejected / No Trade Does not meet the model’s minimum setup-quality threshold.

To prevent the dashboard from becoming crowded, only the top-ranked ideas per mode can receive the Highest Priority label.

6. Entry, Stop-Loss, and Target Logic

Entries are trigger-based. A BUY setup requires the price to break above the entry trigger. A SELL setup requires the price to break below the entry trigger. The model favors confirmation rather than blind entry.

  • Entry: Trigger level based on price action near the latest candle.
  • Stop-loss: Uses structure-aware ATR placement, normally beyond a recent swing high or swing low with a volatility buffer.
  • Target: Uses risk-based target logic, generally around 2R in the newer professional model.
  • Position size: Adjusts based on risk per trade and maximum allocation rules.
Wider structure-aware stops may reduce position quantity. That is intentional. The goal is to control rupee/dollar risk, not force the same share quantity on every setup.

7. Performance Tracking

The performance dashboard reads the scanner logs and tracks whether an idea remained pending, triggered, hit stop-loss first, or hit target first. This creates a feedback loop to evaluate whether the scanner logic is producing useful candidates over time.

  • Current Price: Latest available price when the scanner workflow runs.
  • Stop Away: Distance from current price to stop-loss, shown in price and percentage terms.
  • Target Away: Distance from current price to target, shown in price and percentage terms.
  • First Hit: Indicates whether stop or target was touched first after entry.
  • Result: Win, Loss, Active, Pending, or Ambiguous depending on the available candle data.
  • R: Risk-adjusted outcome, where +1R means the trade gained one unit of planned risk and -1R means the stop-loss was hit.

8. Same-Candle Ambiguity

Sometimes the available candle data may show that entry, stop, and target were all touched within the same candle. In that case, the scanner may not know the exact sequence of events. Instead of incorrectly marking it as a definite win or loss, the performance tracker may label it as ambiguous.

This is especially important for daily candles and sometimes for 15-minute candles, because candle OHLC data does not always reveal the order in which price levels were touched.

9. Practical Use Rules

  • Do not treat the scanner as an automatic buy/sell engine.
  • Do not enter before the trigger level breaks with confirmation.
  • Do not interpret conviction score as probability of profit.
  • Review liquidity, news, earnings, macro events, and broader market conditions before acting.
  • Respect position sizing. A wider stop should reduce quantity, not increase risk.
  • Use the performance dashboard to identify whether the model is improving or deteriorating over time.

10. Key Limitations

No scanner can remove market risk. Technical setups can fail because of earnings, macro news, sudden sector rotation, liquidity shocks, false breakouts, gaps, and broad-market reversals. Data from public sources such as Yahoo Finance may be delayed, unavailable, or revised.

The scanner should be treated as a structured research assistant, not a substitute for professional financial advice, discretionary judgment, or independent risk management.
